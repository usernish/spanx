How to Use Anti-Freeze Coolant in Your Vehicle

Step-by-Step Guide

1

Check Specifications

Refer to your vehicle's owner manual for the recommended coolant type and mixing ratio.

2

Prepare Your Vehicle

Ensure the engine is cool before opening the radiator cap to avoid burns.

3

Drain Old Coolant

Locate the drain valve under the radiator and carefully remove the old coolant.

4

Flush the System

Use distilled water to flush out any remaining debris or old coolant before refilling.

5

Add New Coolant

Pour the correct mix of anti-freeze coolant and distilled water into the radiator.

6

Check Coolant Level

Run the engine, check for air bubbles, and top up if necessary.

Maintenance Tips

  • ⏱️ Change Intervals: Replace coolant every 40,000-100,000 km (depending on manufacturer recommendations)
  • 📊 Regular Checks: Check coolant levels monthly to prevent overheating
  • ⚠️ Warning Signs: Look for overheating, leaks, or discolored coolant
  • ♻️ Disposal: Dispose of used coolant at an authorized recycling center
